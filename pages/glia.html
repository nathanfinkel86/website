Glia

A playful collaborative space for making music together. Visual programming that feels sensory and intuitive. Took it on the road for December 2025 shows—where creative tools become an extension of play.

Listen / watch

What it is

Glia is a collaborative music-making environment. Multiple people build sound together in real-time—visually, intuitively, without needing to code or have prior experience. It feels playful, not technical.

Drag and drop nodes, watch them connect, hear the result immediately. Runs as a plugin, standalone, or on the web. Real-time synchronization means everyone's changes show up instantly—it's like jamming, but with sound design.

I contribute and early-test it in live performance. It's become part of how I think about making space for creative collaboration.

How it works (technical side)

Audio engine

Real-time sound synthesis and effects. What you build is what you hear, instantly.

Visual interface

Nodes and connections. No hidden menus or complex workflows—it's intuitive to explore and play with.

Real-time sync

Everyone's changes sync instantly. No lag, no conflicts. Multiple people can edit the same graph at once.

How I use it

On stage

Integrated into my live rig. Responds to MIDI, audio input, whatever's happening around it. Reliable in the moment.

With other tools

Works alongside hardware synths, guitar, field recordings. One more sensory layer in the performance.

With visuals (next)

Building audio-reactive visuals that respond to what's coming out of Glia. Sound and sight together.

Recent work

Live shows
