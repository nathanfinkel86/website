Glia (neural-network instrument)

Glia runs as a VST in a DAW. I integrate it with a live rig for real-time MIDI/audio interaction (successful UK shows in Dec 2025).

Listen / watch

What it is

Glia is a collaborative project: a neural-network-based instrument environment that runs as a VST plugin. It has two main engines—Groove (rhythmic) and Harmony (melodic/harmonic)—that respond to live audio and MIDI input.

I didn't build Glia, but I've integrated it into my performance workflow and taken it on the road for live shows.

How I use it

Real-time MIDI/audio

Groove Engine responds to audio input; Harmony Engine modulates/arpeggiates MIDI input from keys/synth.

Live integration

Runs in Ableton Live alongside the rest of my rig—hardware synths, guitar, field recordings.

Visual mapping (in progress)

Developing VDMX-based workflows for audio-reactive visuals.

Recent work

December 2025

UK live shows

Took Glia on the road for a series of live performances. The shows tested the integration of the neural engines with the rest of my rig—reliable routing, real-time flexibility, and audience response.
